Saturn: The Ringed Giant

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is easily recognizable by its stunning system of rings. It is a gas giant, and its composition is mainly hydrogen and helium. The rings of Saturn are made of ice and rock and stretch out over 175,000 kilometers from the planet, although they are only about 1 km thick.

Despite being less dense than water, Saturn is incredibly massive. The planet has 82 known moons, with Titan being the largest. Titan is larger than the planet Mercury and has a thick atmosphere composed mainly of nitrogen.
