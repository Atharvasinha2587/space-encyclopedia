Uranus: The Tilted Ice Giant

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, and it's one of the most intriguing planets in the solar system due to its extreme tilt. Unlike most planets, Uranus rotates on its side, meaning that its poles experience 42 years of continuous daylight followed by 42 years of darkness.

Uranus is a gas giant, composed mostly of hydrogen, helium, and ices such as water, ammonia, and methane. This gives the planet its distinct blue-green hue. It has 27 known moons, and its rings are faint but still visible from space. The planet's extreme tilt and icy composition make it a fascinating subject of study.
